What is Public Relations?

Public Relations (PR) refers to strategies that manage people’s feelings and perspectives toward a brand or, company. the main goals are to outspread crucial company news or events, maintain a brand reputation, and put a beneficial influence e on negative events to prevent their fallout. PR may develop in the form of a company press release, interviews, social media posting, or any other venue. PR is crucial to a company's success, notably when shares in the company are publicly traded and the value of a share depends on the people's confidence in a company or brand.

What is a Marketing Strategy?

A marketing strategy is a plan with the goal of achieving a company's primary goals by comprehending customers' necessities and creating a sustainable competitive benefit. In addition, a marketing strategy can help all of the company’s activities on track and essentially boost the success of any company.

PR vs Marketing Strategy

Marketing is often more focused on driving sellings, promoting products, and guaranteeing financial success. On the other hand, public relations is frequently more about managing the reputation and image of a company or brand. Both tracks may partake in similar types of activities. For instance, both may require interaction with customers to collect feedback. From a more direct marketing perspective, this information is used to better understand selling trends, product demands, and ways to generate more profits. Meanwhile, from the public relations lens, this information is used to comprehend customer satisfaction, ensure customers' happiness, and convincing that any dissatisfaction will be handled quickly.
